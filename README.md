# BacVacDB: A Knowledgebase of Bacterial Vaccines

Welcome to the official repository for BacVacDB, a comprehensive and manually curated knowledgebase of bacterial vaccines for human use. This resource is designed to support researchers and the pharmaceutical industry in understanding, comparing, and developing vaccines against human bacterial diseases.

Database URL: https://webs.iiitd.edu.in/raghava/bacvacdb/

## Citation

Gupta, S., Sharma, N., Naorem, L. D., Jain, S., & Raghava, G. P. S. (2022).

## About the Database

BacVacDB is the first database solely dedicated to bacterial vaccines for human use. It consolidates scattered information from literature, regulatory agencies, and clinical trial databases into a single centralized platform, enabling systematic exploration of vaccine types, efficacy, mechanisms of action, administration routes, and approval status.

The database integrates information from:

* Literature (Google Scholar, PubMed)
* Regulatory sources (US FDA, WHO, CDC, European Medicines Agency)
* Clinical trial registry (ClinicalTrials.gov)
* Drug and disease databases (DrugBank, TTD, MalaCards, Swiss-Prot, NCBI)
* Electronic medicines compendium (EMC)


## Key Features

Curated Dataset

* 371 total vaccine entries
* 167 approved vaccines
* 204 vaccines in clinical trials
* Coverage across 30 human bacterial diseases
* Data catalogued across 33 annotation fields per entry

Extensive Disease Coverage

* Diphtheria (66 entries, ~22% of total)
* Tetanus (36 approved vaccines)
* Pertussis, typhoid, cholera, meningococcal disease, pneumococcal disease, and more

Vaccine Type Diversity

* Combination (131), conjugate (64), inactivated (56), recombinant (31), live attenuated, toxoid, subunit/polysaccharide, viral vector-based, and gene-based vaccines

Rich Annotations
Each entry includes:

* Vaccine name, type, phase (approved/clinical), dosage, and formulation
* Mechanism of action, efficacy, and side effects
* Age range, adjuvant details, and route of administration
* Manufacturer name, country, and year of manufacture
* Bacteria name, disease, strain, and nucleic acid content
* Approval agency, export status, repurposing, and post-vaccination details


## Overview

BacVacDB provides curated data along with:

* Vaccine approval and clinical trial status
* Route of administration profiles (intramuscular, oral, subcutaneous, intradermal, intranasal, intravenous, percutaneous)
* Vaccine type classification with pros, cons, and examples
* Historical timeline of bacterial vaccine development (1879–2021)
* Cross-references to DrugBank, TTD, MalaCards, Swiss-Prot, PubChem, NCBI, and Ontobee


### Vaccine Types Covered

* Live attenuated (e.g., BCG for tuberculosis, Vivotif for typhoid)
* Toxoids (e.g., Tdvax for diphtheria and tetanus)
* Subunit/polysaccharide (e.g., Pneumovax 23, Mencevax ACWY)
* Conjugate (e.g., Prevnar 20, MenHibrix)
* Inactivated/killed (e.g., Dukoral for cholera, Plague vaccine USP)
* Recombinant (e.g., Bexsero, Trumenba for meningococcal disease)
* Viral vector-based (e.g., TB/FLU-01L, NasoShield)
* Combination/polyvalent (e.g., DTap, Tdap, Pediarix, Infanrix-hexa)
* Gene-based (no currently approved human vaccines; included for clinical pipeline)


### Data Retrieval Tools

* **Basic Search** — Query any field by default (vaccine name, disease, bacteria, administration route, vaccine type); results downloadable in CSV format
* **Advanced Search** — Multi-field queries with Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT); results downloadable in CSV format
* **Browse** — By bacterial disease, bacteria name, vaccine type, or route of administration
* **External Links** — Cross-linked disease, adjuvant, and resource information via DrugBank, TTD, MalaCards, Swiss-Prot, NCBI, PubChem, and Ontobee
* **General Information** — Statistics, data submission page for new vaccine entries, and help documentation

### Limitations

* Vaccine target information for the causative bacteria is not currently included
* Coverage is limited to human bacterial vaccines only (not animal hosts)


## Applications

* Novel bacterial vaccine candidate development
* Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) research and vaccine-based intervention strategies
* Comparative analysis of vaccine types, efficacy, and approval status
* Clinical trial monitoring and regulatory submission support
* Vaccine repurposing research
* Public health and epidemiological studies on vaccine-preventable bacterial diseases
